# 213, 16A

An interactive companion to K.S. Sarkaria's paper [*"213, 16A" and Mathematics*](https://kssarkaria.org/docs/213.pdf) (2010).

The paper is a guided tour through the author's house at 213, 16A in Chandigarh, in which each architectural feature embodies a piece of mathematics — the golden rectangle in the front-door mural, an icosahedron as three golden rectangles in the water pond, the five regular solids via the rooftop pyramid, Euler's V−E+F on the office-window painting, and a driveway whose red-brick pattern spells out `11010101` — the binary for 213.

This site rebuilds the tour as an interactive experience at [213.kssarkaria.org](https://213.kssarkaria.org).
